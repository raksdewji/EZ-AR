CPSC 481 P1 User Research: EZ-AR

Project Idea: The objective of this project is to create a mobile application that acts as a potential marketing tool for businesses. The mobile application will utilize an AR engine (EasyAR). We hope to build a system where users can utilize their android mobile devices to scan an image related to a product, and a corresponding AR animation will pop up. This animation will serve as a good way to grab the user’s attention, and inform them about the product being advertised. This will also allow companies/businesses to market themselves and attract more clients in a unique way. In order for this to work, marketers or product providers will need to scan their advertisements and create an animation that should appear when potential buyers scan their advertisement.

Stakeholders and Users: The main stakeholders are the different businesses that will utilize the mobile application to market their product, as well as the users (potential clients of businesses) that will interact with the mobile application to learn more about a business’ product. The business owners are expected to provide the promotion image, animation and video of their product to the developers of the mobile application. We will then upload the animation/video so that it will be an augmented reality video when users of the application scan the product’s image. This application can be utilized by businesses from any industry. Clients of businesses are the primary users of this application, as they are the ones being advertised to by the businesses. Since the application just requires the user to point their phone to an image it is very easy to learn and use. The user doesn’t require too much background knowledge other than the fact that they need to know if the image can be scanned and converted to an AR animation/video.

Research Methods: We decided to conduct two user research methods: Fly on the wall (Look) -> Interview (Ask). We chose Fly on the Wall because it allows us to monitor and make key observations on how a user may interact with a very raw, beta version of our application. This is especially effective since it helps us find out what actions users take that they don’t realize they are doing; this can ignite inspiration for possible future implementations. To complement this method, we decided we will conduct an interview with the users and ask them questions about their experience with the app at its current state, and it’s future potential. In addition, we also asked users questions about the app from the perspective of a user and a stakeholder. This method is important for getting user feedback and understanding the user’s mental model during the Fly on the Wall process.

Fly on the Wall - User Experiences

User 1 (Novice experience with app interfaces - Non-CPSC Background):

  • Tried to click the record button to see what it does; the button currently has no function so this made user confused.
  • Saw an AR and moved phone around with the video still playing in the same spot of the still image
  • Found the AR aspect interesting
  • App crashed when viewing one of the full screen video advertisements, there is currently no error message so user did not know what to do next.
  • Confused about why only the hexagon was being scanned, not the full screen.

User 2 (Expert experience with app interfaces - CPSC Background):  

  • User moved the phone back and forth to see the effects of the AR
  • Finds sound aspect of the AR video interesting
  • Doesn't know what recording button is used for, not clear
  • Didn’t know when to rotate phone when the full screen video played; this also lead to the app crashing.
  • Found the system was responsive enough for a prototype.

Interview - Questions

  1. What are your first impressions?
  2. Was it easy to use/understand? Rate it on a scale from 1-5:

  1. Rate the aesthetics of the app on a scale from 1-5:

  3. As a consumer would you use this?

Interview - User Responses

User 1:

  1. User was impressed and mind blown with the augmented reality feature. User was interested by the concept of a video playing on top of an image through the mobile app.
  2. It was confusing at first; the user didn’t know the app was mainly intended for advertisements. He also didn’t know what the functionality of the app was at first. But once he used it for a bit, he got more comfortable with the app and it became easier to use. User believes more help messages can be convenient for novice users. User rated it 2.5 out of 5 in usability.
  3. User rates the aesthetics a 4 out of 5. Enjoys the simplicity of the current interface.
  4. The initial start up of the app should make the app’s intentions and purpose more obvious. User thinks that customers should have the option to look at more information.
  5. Yes, would use it to advertise because is a very convenient way to get information about the product. It’s a different way to gain a customer’s attention with the AR aspect.
  6. Yes, it is a good way for the consumer to have more interaction with the company advertising their product and will be interested in the product being advertised.

User 2:

  1. User liked it, AR is a new idea and can implement cool features with the advertisements. Believes this idea allows the advertisements to be more interactive. Simple to just point and look at the ad to enjoy the AR. A bit confusing at first but easy to get a hang of it.
  2. User said that some advertisements don’t carry the same interactive feature, for example, a test image played a video which covered the whole screen. Takes away the ability to move your phone around and play around with the video. User rated it 5 out of 5 in usability.
  3. User rates the aesthetics a 3.5-4 out of 5. He doesn’t like the hexagon shape, believes a square/rectangle/full screen would be more appropriate
  4. The viewfinder should be the entire screen. There should be a recommendation of what to do with the camera and where to point the scanner.
  5. Yes, I would use this because it helps advertising my product in a more interactive way. Also gives an alternative to showing the product without having to come into a store or visit a website.
  6. Yes, I would use this as a consumer because I wouldn’t have to do extra work such as searching for product information or a product promotion video; all of it can be done through the app.

Method Reflection

What Went Well

The two methods that we chose to conduct (Fly on the Wall -> Interview), synergized really well together and overall gave our project members valuable information about the application, and what important things to pursue further when developing a refined version of our app interface. Observing the users without interfering showed us things that we didn’t see ourselves,  and we were able to segway into a great interview process afterwards.

What Went Poorly

When conducting the Fly on the Wall method, the app crashed when rotating the screen to view the video. Since there was no error message, this user did not know what to do and the conductors of the test had to intervene and help the user resolve the issue.

What Would You Do Differently

Before conducting the Fly on the Wall method, we would provide the participants with a more detailed summary of what the current version of our application does, as well as a little bit of background knowledge about the purpose of the app. Our initial strategy (very broad run-down of the app) led to a little bit of confusion during the Fly on the Wall process, and the results clearly showed in the interview sessions with the users.

System Task Descriptions

  1. Clients/Customers of businesses (Users) will use the viewfinder to scan a product image(s). Once the image is scanned, an interactive AR promotion animation/video will plays over the image.
  2. Stakeholders (Business Owners) will provide their image and advertisement video through a “Business Owner Account”.
  3. User has the ability to create a profile. They can share advertisement images/videos.
  4. User has the ability to record their viewfinder.
  5. User has the option to see a list/map of scannable images within their area.
  6. User has the ability to explore more about the product (via product website, google search etc.) through clicking a side-icon.
  7. User has the ability to expand the video into full screen by double-tapping the AR video.
